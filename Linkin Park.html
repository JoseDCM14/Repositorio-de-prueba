In 1996, the musical band Linkin Park was formed in California, United States, whose musical genre is a mixture of metal and hardcore. The idea of forming a musical group came from Brad Delson, Mike Shinoda and Rob Bourdon, who studied together in high school and also agreed in terms of tastes in musical style. They were soon joined by Dave Farrell and Joe Hahn.

When they had completed the formation of the group with five members, the members thought it would be nice to also have a vocalist within it, so they told Mark Wakefield if he was interested in being a member of the group, accepting it immediately. Soon, and although with few resources, the group began recording their first single, which was called "Xero", in 1996. This first recording did not have the successes expected by the band and they did not get any record contract. This fact caused that the singer, Wakefield, left the band to continue with his studies.

Again without a singer, the band began to look for people with a good voice for the group, until Chester Bennington arrived, a young man with experience in other groups and with a good voice very according to the characteristics of the group.

At that time the band was called "Hybrid Theory", but because in Europe there was already a musical group with a very similar name, they decided to change it to "Linkin Park".

Although the band released good music, they could not find any record label that would support them, until, in 1999, Jeff Blue, vice president of Warner Bros. Records, after listening to them, decided to give them a chance to start record material. The band began to edit their first album "Hybrid Theory",which was released in 2000 and from which several singles were extracted, all of them with considerable success, obtaining the songs "In the End" and "One Step Closer". , gold records in no time. This album obtained the second position in the lists of Billboard 200 and was the 7th more sold album of that year. It also achieved diamond discand multiple platinum ratings in the United States and Europe, respectively.

It was followed by Reanimation (an album of remixes and old material from the band) and in 2002 Meteora with singles like "Somewhere I belong" and "Breaking the habit". After finishing the tour of the latter, there was a stoppage for the band where its members made several collaborations outside the formation. They are back to work on a Collision Course remix album with the collaboration of rapper Jay-Z, where the remix of the single "Numb / Encore" will be part of the soundtrack of the movie "Corruption in Miami".

In 2006 they released their album Minutes to midnight, whose single "What I've done" was part of the O.S.T. from "Transformers". The album, named after the doomsday clock, reached the top of the Billboard charts and went platinum.It is an album that is far from the first and is far from their metal style.

In 2009 they became part of the O.S.T. of the new installment of "Transformers 2: Revenge of the Fallen", with his song "New divide" and in 2010 his new work entitled "A Thousand suns" is released, a concept album that revolves around the fear of a nuclear war (the title makes mention of the Hindu quote that Oppenheimer cited after the creation of the atomic bomb) and that represents a radical change in the sound of the band, where they experiment more with sounds and mixes. The single Iridescent will also be part of the soundtrack for "Transformers 3: Dark Side
of the Moon".

Living Things was their next studio album, shot in between the tour, as was also the case with Meteora, and the one that is most reminiscent of the band's first album, in which they mix the tastes of all the members of the band. Their single "Castle of glass" will be part of the music for the video game "Medal of Honor: Warfighter".

Their latest works The Hunting party and One more light have received all kinds of criticism, although it is true that the band follows the path of experimenting with sounds and mixes, these last works seem to depart a bit from their first albums.

On June 20, 2017, Chester Bennington's body was found at his home in Palos Verdes, leaving many fans orphans of his characteristic throaty voice and a group that is now undertaking a change without its iconic singer.
